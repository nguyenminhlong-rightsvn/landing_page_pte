Titles and Subheadings - Using Paragraphs and Main Ideas

Introduction

Titles and subheadings are like road signs. They help you know what the passage or section is about before you even start reading. In the IELTS Reading test, they can save you time and give you a better idea of what’s important in the text.

Titles tell you the overall subject of the passage.

With user-centered approach, the goals was to create an intuitive interface for effortless financial management while incorporating gamification.

By understanding these, you can quickly find the main ideas and focus on what the questions are asking.

How to Use Titles and Subheadings Effectively

Here are some easy steps to make the most of titles and subheadings when reading:

Start with the title: Look at the title of the passage first. This will give you a general idea of the topic. Ask yourself, “What is this passage about?”
Skim the subheadings: Before reading, quickly skim through the subheadings. Subheadings divide the text into smaller sections, helping you understand the main points of each part.
Link subheadings to the questions: As you read the questions, remember the subheadings. Each subheading can guide you to the right section of the passage to answer the questions more easily.
Check for keywords: Subheadings often contain important keywords related to the questions. Look for words that match the question or hint at an answer.
Example Task: Using Titles and Subheadings

Imagine you have a passage with the title: "The Benefits of Exercise on Mental Health".

Now, the subheadings are:

Physical Activity and Brain Function
Exercise and Stress Reduction
The Role of Exercise in Managing Anxiety
How Often Should You Exercise?

By just reading the title and subheadings, you already know that this passage is about how exercise helps the brain and reduces stress and anxiety.

You can use this information to understand the overall structure and where you might find answers to questions about brain function, stress, and exercise routines.

Common Queries Answered

Titles and subheadings help structure the writing, making it easier for readers to follow the main ideas. They guide the reader through different sections of the text, clarify the content of each part, and help identify the key themes and topics being discussed.

Subheadings provide a brief summary of what each paragraph or section will cover, which helps the reader quickly grasp the main ideas. This also allows the writer to stay focused on specific topics, ensuring each paragraph has a clear and distinct purpose.

Each paragraph should focus on one main idea, which is often introduced in the topic sentence. The rest of the paragraph supports this idea with details, examples, or explanations. This structure helps the reader understand the flow of the argument or discussion clearly.
